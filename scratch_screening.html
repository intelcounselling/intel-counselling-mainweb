INTEL COUNSELLING – WEBSITE DESIGNER INSTRUCTIONS

(Mental Health Screening Tools Integration)

1️⃣ OVERALL PURPOSE (IMPORTANT)

These questionnaires are self-screening tools, not diagnostic tests.

Primary goals:

  • Increase user self-awareness
  • Build trust & emotional safety
  • Ethically guide users to book counselling sessions
  • Maintain mental-health compliance & ethics

2️⃣ GENERAL FLOW (FOR ALL QUESTIONNAIRES)

Step 1: Pre-Questionnaire Prompt Page

Each test must begin with:

  • Purpose of the test
  • Time required (2–5 minutes)
  • Assurance of confidentiality
  • “Not a diagnosis” disclaimer
  • Clear Start button

👉 No pop-ups. Keep it calm and clean.

Step 2: Questionnaire Page

  • One question per screen OR 4–5 per page
  • Use radio buttons / sliders
  • Show progress bar
  • Use consistent response scale
  • “Next” and “Back” buttons allowed

⚠️ Do NOT allow skipping questions.

Step 3: Auto-Scoring (Backend)

  • Score automatically
  • Apply reverse scoring where required (e.g., PSS-10)
  • Categorize result into:
    • Minimal / Mild / Moderate / Severe
    • OR Low / Moderate / High (as provided)

⚠️ Do NOT show raw score numbers to clients unless mentioned.

Step 4: Results Page (MOST IMPORTANT)

Each results page must include:

  1. Result label (e.g., Moderate Stress)
  2. Short empathetic explanation
  3. Clear next-step suggestion
  4. ONE primary CTA button
  5. Disclaimer at bottom

3️⃣ QUESTIONNAIRES TO BE IMPLEMENTED

✅ 1. Depression – PHQ-9

  • Use standard PHQ-9 wording
  • Scoring: 0–27
  • Severity levels:
    • 0–4 Minimal
    • 5–9 Mild
    • 10–14 Moderate
    • 15–19 Moderately Severe
    • 20–27 Severe
  • If Item 9 > 0, show crisis message

CTA examples:

  • Mild → Book Preventive Session
  • Moderate+ → Book Counselling Session

✅ 2. Anxiety – GAD-7

  • Use standard GAD-7
  • Scoring: 0–21
  • Levels:
    • 0–4 Minimal
    • 5–9 Mild
    • 10–14 Moderate
    • 15–21 Severe

CTA:

  • Mild+ → Book Anxiety Support Session

✅ 3. Stress – PSS-10

  • Use standard PSS-10
  • Reverse score items 4, 5, 7, 8
  • Total score: 0–40
  • Levels:
    • 0–13 Low
    • 14–26 Moderate
    • 27–40 High

CTA:

  • Moderate+ → Book Stress Management Session

✅ 4. Sleep Hygiene – InterSleek Sleep Checklist

  • 10 items
  • Scale: 0–4
  • Total score: 0–40
  • Levels:
    • 0–10 Healthy
    • 11–20 Mild concern
    • 21–30 Moderate difficulty
    • 31–40 Severe issue

CTA:

  • Moderate+ → Book Sleep Support Counselling

✅ 5. Smartphone Addiction – SAS-SV (or Intel version)

  • Likert scale
  • Gender-specific cut-off
  • Show:
    • Low Risk
    • High Risk

CTA:

  • High Risk → Start Digital Balance Counselling

4️⃣ CTA BUTTON RULES (VERY IMPORTANT)

  • Only ONE main CTA button per result
  • CTA text should be:
    • Supportive
    • Non-threatening
    • Action-oriented

CTA destinations:

  • Online booking page
  • WhatsApp chat
  • Call button (for Moderate & Severe only)

5️⃣ TONE & DESIGN STYLE

  • Calm
  • Professional
  • Warm
  • No red flashing warnings
  • No alarming words like “danger”, “disorder”, “illness”

Use:

  • Soft colors
  • Simple fonts
  • Plenty of white space

1️⃣ DEPRESSION SCREENING – PHQ-9

Questionnaire (Standardized – use exact wording)

Over the last 2 weeks, how often have you been bothered by the following problems?

Options for all questions:
0 – Not at all
1 – Several days
2 – More than half the days
3 – Nearly every day

  1. Little interest or pleasure in doing things
  2. Feeling down, depressed, or hopeless
  3. Trouble falling or staying asleep, or sleeping too much
  4. Feeling tired or having little energy
  5. Poor appetite or overeating
  6. Feeling bad about yourself — or that you are a failure
  7. Trouble concentrating
  8. Moving or speaking slowly / being restless
  9. Thoughts that you would be better off dead or of hurting yourself

Sleep Hygiene Self-Check

(InterSleek™ – based on the Sleep Hygiene Index framework)

🔹 PRE-QUESTIONNAIRE PROMPT

(What clients see before starting)

Sleep Hygiene Self-Check

This short questionnaire helps you reflect on your sleep habits and bedtime routines.
There are no right or wrong answers.

Please respond honestly based on your usual behavior over the past 2 weeks.

⚠️ This is a self-reflection and awareness tool, not a medical or psychiatric diagnosis.

Click Start when you’re ready.

🔹 RESPONSE SCALE

(Same for all questions)

  • 0 – Never
  • 1 – Rarely
  • 2 – Sometimes
  • 3 – Often
  • 4 – Always

🔹 QUESTIONS

(Sleep Hygiene Index – Adapted for Online Use)

  1. I go to bed at different times each night.
  2. I wake up at different times each morning.
  3. I use my phone, laptop, or television in bed before sleeping.
  4. I consume caffeine (tea, coffee, energy drinks) close to bedtime.
  5. I go to bed feeling stressed, worried, or emotionally upset.
  6. I stay in bed even when I am unable to fall asleep.
  7. I take long daytime naps.
  8. My sleep environment is uncomfortable (noise, light, temperature, or disturbance).
  9. I think, overthink, or worry a lot while trying to fall asleep.
  10. I use my bed for activities other than sleeping (studying, scrolling, eating).

🔹 SCORING INSTRUCTIONS

(For website backend – not shown to clients)

  • Add the scores for all 10 items
  • Total score range: 0 – 40

Higher scores indicate poorer sleep hygiene.

Stress Self-Check

(Perceived Stress Scale – PSS-10)

🔹 PRE-QUESTIONNAIRE PROMPT

(What clients see before starting)

Stress Self-Check

This questionnaire helps you understand how stressful you have found your life during the past month.

There are no right or wrong answers.
Please respond based on your personal experience, not how you think you should feel.

⚠️ This is a self-reflection and awareness tool, not a medical or psychological diagnosis.

Click Start when you’re ready.

🔹 RESPONSE SCALE

(Same for all questions)

  • 0 – Never
  • 1 – Almost Never
  • 2 – Sometimes
  • 3 – Fairly Often
  • 4 – Very Often

🔹 QUESTIONS

(Perceived Stress Scale – 10 items)

During the last month, how often have you felt…

  1. Upset because of something that happened unexpectedly?
  2. Unable to control important things in your life?
  3. Nervous or stressed?
  4. Confident about your ability to handle personal problems?
  5. That things were going your way?
  6. Unable to cope with all the things that you had to do?
  7. Able to control irritations in your life?
  8. That you were on top of things?
  9. Angered because of things that were outside of your control?
  10. That difficulties were piling up so high that you could not overcome them?

🔹 SCORING INSTRUCTIONS

(For website backend – not shown to clients)

Step 1: Reverse-score the following items:

  • Items 4, 5, 7, and 8

Reverse scoring:

  • 0 → 4
  • 1 → 3
  • 2 → 2
  • 3 → 1
  • 4 → 0

Step 2:

  • Add scores for all 10 items

Total score range: 0 – 40

Higher scores indicate higher perceived stress.

🔹 STRESS LEVEL INTERPRETATION & CLIENT PROMPTS

🟢 0–13 | Low Perceived Stress

What client sees:

Your responses suggest low stress levels.
You appear to be managing daily demands reasonably well.
Continue practicing healthy coping and self-care strategies.

CTA Button:
➡️ Explore Wellness Resources

🟡 14–26 | Moderate Perceived Stress

What client sees:

Your responses indicate moderate stress levels.
You may be experiencing ongoing pressure that could affect your emotional well-being if left unaddressed.
Learning stress-management strategies can help restore balance.

CTA Button:
➡️ Book a Stress Management Counselling Session

🔴 27–40 | High Perceived Stress

What client sees:

Your responses suggest high stress levels that may be impacting your mood, concentration, sleep, or daily functioning.
Professional support is strongly recommended to help you manage stress more effectively and prevent burnout.

CTA Button:
➡️ Speak to a Counsellor About Stress Today

🔹 GLOBAL DISCLAIMER

(Shown at the bottom of the results page)

The Stress Self-Check (PSS-10) is intended for self-awareness and early support only.
It does not replace professional diagnosis or treatment.
If stress symptoms persist or worsen, seeking professional support is advised.
